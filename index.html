Poem About the Internet

Dial-up, not dial-up,
the tangled cords that connect me to you:
It is 1998, and I am too young
to type
my secret, hidden confessions.

The Internet was made for the world to uncover.

Dial-up, not dial-up,
the same pulse:
replacing radio
replacing television.
A monitor yells:
You've Got Mail!
You've got my message,
You've discovered who I am.
